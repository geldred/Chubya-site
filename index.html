On-Course Quick Reference

League Rules

The fast-read version for checking a ruling on the tee, in the fairway, or while the group is waiting. Keep it moving, keep it fair, and default to good-faith judgment.

1

Philosophy of Play

Core principles

The Chubya Cup Series is a casual, competition-friendly golf league designed to prioritize pace of play, fairness, and enjoyment over strict adherence to formal tournament rules. Integrity is expected; rigidity is not.

2A

Red Stakes Everywhere

OB treated as red penalty areas
  • All out-of-bounds and hazard areas are treated as red penalty areas
  • Players may take lateral relief within two club lengths from the point of entry, no closer to the hole
  • Penalty: one stroke
  • No stroke-and-distance requirement for OB

Purpose: eliminates re-teeing and speeds up play

2B

Divot Relief

Fairway only
  • If a ball comes to rest in a divot or clearly damaged turf area in the fairway, the player may lift, clean, and place within one club length
  • The ball must remain in the fairway and not be moved closer to the hole

This is not a general preferred-lies rule. It applies only to divots or abnormal damage

2C

Breakfast Ball

First tee only
  • Each player is allowed one mulligan on the first tee shot only
  • It must be declared immediately after the first shot
  • The second shot is the ball in play. No further mulligans
2D

Lost Ball Relief

Pace-of-play relief
  • If a ball is likely lost and the search exceeds about two minutes, drop near the estimated area
  • Penalty: one stroke
2E

Gimme Putts

Conceded inside grip length
  • Inside putter-grip length, or the official Chubya Gimme StickTM, putts may be conceded
  • In competitive matches, opponents may require completion
3

Etiquette & Pace of Play

Group rhythm
  • Play ready golf
  • Keep pace with the group ahead
  • Limit ball searches
  • Respect playing partners and course conditions
4

Scoring Integrity

Good-faith scorekeeping
  • Players are responsible for honestly reporting scores
  • No vanity handicaps or score manipulation
  • When in doubt, take the reasonable, good-faith interpretation
5

Dispute Resolution

Chairman authority
  • On-course rulings are decided by group consensus
  • League disputes are resolved by the Chairman or Rule Czar
  • The Chairman has sole and final authority over all rulings and may overrule any decision
6

The Spirit of the Chubya Cup

League philosophy
  • Keep rounds moving
  • Reduce frustration
  • Encourage participation

Maintain competitive fun without over-policing rules. If a rule slows things down or kills the vibe, you're probably doing it wrong.
